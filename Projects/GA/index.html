This mechanical system was developed as part of our senior capstone project in collaboration with General Atomics and UC San Diego. Our team designed and prototyped an automated target handling device for inertial fusion energy (IFE) reactors ‚Äî capable of precisely loading delicate fusion targets at high speeds and under vacuum conditions.

The autoloader system is composed of three major subsystems: 1) the funnel and gate system, 2) rotary disk transfer system, and 3) impeller-driven loading system. Each part was precision-manufactured using CNC machining and laser-cutting processes, with all motion driven via stepper motors and actuated via aluminum linkages.

This project involved a multidisciplinary approach including: system design, CAD modeling, mechanical fabrication, motion control via stepper motors, and the creation of a vacuum-compatible environment for target loading. The prototype helped pave the way for a more automated IFE reactor workflow at General Atomics.
