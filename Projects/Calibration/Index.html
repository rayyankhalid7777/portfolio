Calibrating Sensors Lab

2024 | UC San Diego - MAE 170

In this project, we calibrated two key sensors: an **ultrasonic sensor** to measure distances and an **accelerometer** to measure accelerations. The main objective was to establish the relationship between the sensor readings and physical quantities by performing calibration tests.

The ultrasonic sensor was calibrated by measuring the time of flight of sound waves at different known distances and correlating the results. We used an Arduino to control the sensor and collect data, and the results were plotted to obtain the calibration curve.

For the accelerometer, we performed a similar process, capturing the voltage output while varying acceleration. The voltage output was then plotted against the known acceleration to establish the relationship. Both experiments used MATLAB to visualize and analyze the data.
