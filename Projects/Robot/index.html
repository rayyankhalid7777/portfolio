Robot Contest - Scuff Bot

2024 | UC San Diego - MAE 3

Team Website | Report

The Scuff Bot was designed for the MAE 3 Robot Contest, where our challenge was to design a robot that could efficiently move objects in a limited space. The robot features a rear-wheel drivetrain powered by two geared motors, and its chassis was optimized for speed and efficiency. Our solution incorporated dual L-arms to scoop multiple objects at once, improving efficiency and functionality.

During the competition, we encountered a challenge with the robot's ability to reach distant objects like an iPad. To overcome this, we innovated by adding a crossbow spring shooter design, which allowed the robot to extend its reach and effectively interact with the iPad and other objects.

The Scuff Bot performed well in terms of speed and functionality, but if we had more time, we'd further improve the L-arm design for increased reach and maneuverability. Overall, this project taught us how to combine mechanical design with electrical systems to create a functional and innovative robot that could solve real-world challenges.
